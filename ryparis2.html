Hamburger Of The Week

Time

Level: EasyPrep: 10 mins Yield: 4 Hamburgers
Total: 25 minsCook: 15 mins

BENGAL BURGER

"No more dry, lackluster burgers. These are juicy, and spices
can be easily added or changed to suit anyone's taste. Baste
frequently with your favorite barbeque sauce. If you find the
meat mixture too mushy, just add more bread crumbs until
it forms patties that hold their shape."

Ingredients

  • 24 Oz. Ground chuck
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Spice mix, optional, recipe follows
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 4 slices of cheese, such as cheddar, american cheese
  • 4 soft hamburger style buns, split
  • 4 slices beefsteak tomatos, optional
  • Assorted lettuces and greens, such as Iceburg
  • Assorted mustards, optional
  • Mayonaise optional
  • Ketchup, optional

Spice Mix

  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoon onion powder
  • Hot sauce, to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450‚Ñâ. Place a roasting rack on a foil lined baking sheet in the oven.
  2. Using your hands, break the meat into small pieces and combine evenly but loosely on parchment or wax paper-lined baking sheet. Spread the meat out and season it generously with salt and pepper. if desired, add the spice mix at this time.
  3. Divide the meat into 4 portions (about 6 ounces each). Using your hands, form each portion into a ball-shape by gently tossing it from one hand to the other. (don't over work or press too firmly on the meat.) gently form each portion into a patty about 3 and 1/2 inches wide and 1 inch thick.
  4. Preheat a large cast iron skillet over medium-low heat for five minutes. Raise the heat up to high and add the oil. Add the patties and cook, turning once, until well browned about 2 minutes on each side.
  5. Using a spatula, transfer the hamburgers to the roasting rack in the oven and continue cooking to desired doneness. 8-9 minutes for medium rare, 10-11 minutes for medium, and 13-15 minutes for well-done. if you are using cheese, top the patties for the last couple of minutes for it to melt.
  6. Transfer the burgers to a plate, let rest for a couple of minutes before serving. Meanwhile, toast the hamburger buns, assemble the hamburgers with the condiments and toppings of your choice. Serve.
